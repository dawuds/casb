# Compliance lens review — Palo Alto Prisma Access (SaaS Security / Inline CASB + Aperture API)

Reviewer stance: GRC lead / IS auditor. Frameworks in scope: BNM RMiT (current edition), MAS TRM, HKMA SA-2, ISO/IEC 27017:2015, ISO/IEC 27018:2019, PDPA Malaysia (Act 709, 2010 + 2024 amendments), GDPR, NIST CSF 2.0. Verdict premise: a control that cannot be evidenced to an external auditor with a tamper-evident log artefact and a documented owner does not exist.

## Findings to act on (highest impact first)

1. **No control-mapping column anywhere in the draft.** Every "Supported" line and every one of the 10 configurable policies is asserted with zero linkage to BNM RMiT (e.g. 10.49–10.54 cyber, 11.x cloud, Appendix 8 third-party), ISO/IEC 27017 (CLD.6.3.1 alignment of security management for virtual/physical networks, CLD.9.5.1 segregation in virtual computing environments, CLD.12.4.5 monitoring of cloud services), ISO/IEC 27018 (A.10 PII processor obligations), or NIST CSF 2.0 subcategories (PR.AC-3 / PR.DS-5 / DE.AE-1 / DE.CM-3 / RS.AN-2). Without that mapping the draft is a vendor data-sheet, not a practitioner reference. Add a mandatory "Evidences" cell to every policy and every "Supported" capability. Lines 42–68 and 70–156.

2. **"Supported" is not "auditable".** The draft never asks whether the product emits a per-decision log record with (a) policy ID, (b) policy version/hash, (c) match reason, (d) action taken, (e) user/device identity, (f) timestamp with timezone, (g) tamper protection / WORM. Strata Logging Service retention defaults, immutability, integrity attestation, and export-to-SIEM format are silent across all 10 policies. An auditor presented with "Supported" on inline DLP (line 48) without a sample log artefact rejects it. Add a "Log artefact / evidence path" line to every capability.

3. **SSL/TLS decryption is treated as a deployment toggle, not a legal precondition.** The draft asserts decryption is "required" for activity-level inspection (lines 33, 82, 154) but is silent on the privacy footprint. Under PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act 2010, s.6–s.8 — notice and choice + disclosure) and GDPR (Art. 5(1)(a) lawfulness, Art. 6 legal basis, Art. 88 employment context, recital 47 legitimate-interest balancing), MITM-decrypting an employee's HTTPS session — particularly to consumer GenAI, personal Gmail, banking, healthcare — requires (i) a documented lawful basis, (ii) a DPIA, (iii) a notification + works-council / employee-representative consultation where applicable, (iv) bypass categories for legally privileged / health / financial / authentication traffic. None of this is named. Policy #1 (consumer GenAI block) and #9 (sanctioned-tenant GenAI) both depend on decryption of categories that carry the highest privacy sensitivity. This is the largest single gap in the draft.

4. **No breach / incident notification clock is named anywhere.** Behavior Threats (lines 54, 56) detects compromised accounts and bulk exfil. The draft does not anchor what regulators expect on the back end: BNM RMiT requires notification of a cyber incident to BNM within the timeframe set in the policy (currently the working position is "as soon as possible and no later than within the regulator-specified window — practitioners should confirm against the live RMiT clause before publishing"); PDPA 2024 amendments introduced a mandatory data-breach notification regime (the operative clock and threshold should be cited against JPDP guidance, not asserted from memory); MAS TRM / Notice 644 imposes a 1-hour incident notification for material incidents; HKMA SA-2 / TM-G-1 expects prompt notification; GDPR Art. 33 sets 72 hours to the supervisory authority and Art. 34 "without undue delay" to data subjects where high risk. Detection without a documented downstream notification SLA mapped to each regulator is half a control. Add a regulator-timing appendix.

   Caveat (per global rules): regulatory clocks must be cited to the live source clause at publication time; the figures above are illustrative, not legal advice.

5. **Two-console policy duplication (lines 38, 192) is treated as a UX gripe, not a control-design risk.** When the same logical control ("block external sharing of PII") lives in two policy engines (inline DLP in Strata Cloud Manager + Data Asset policy in Cloud Management Console) with no enforced consistency check, an auditor sampling will find drift, and the drift is invisible from either console alone. ISO/IEC 27001 A.5.36 (compliance with policies) and BNM RMiT change-management expectations require change-control evidence across both. Add a control: a documented quarterly reconciliation between inline and API policy sets, with a sign-off artefact.

6. **"Best-effort" / "supported with caveats" language masks gaps that fail an auditor.** Examples: "Tenant-restriction enforcement varies by app … coverage in the AI Access dictionary should be verified per app at deployment time" (line 146); "Per-app fidelity varies — works well on Google Drive / OneDrive / SharePoint / Box / Dropbox; weaker on Salesforce / ServiceNow" (line 60); "Detection only — automated session-kill … is not a documented native action" (line 56). Each of these is a "control marked operative but not actually enforced" finding in an audit report. Convert each into an explicit residual-risk register entry with a compensating control, not a footnote.

7. **Self-attestation only — no independent test.** Sources (lines 206–222) are 14 vendor-controlled URLs and one independent corroboration (Microsoft Learn on tenant-restriction header insertion, line 219) — and that corroborates a Microsoft-side feature, not the Palo Alto control's auditability. No SOC 2 Type II report reference, no ISO 27001 certification reference for the Prisma Access service, no CSA STAR Level 2 attestation reference, no FedRAMP authorisation reference, no PCI DSS service-provider AoC reference. For a CASB sitting in the data path of regulated workloads, a practitioner reference that lists zero third-party attestations is not promotable. The draft itself flags the absence of independent CVE / breach research (line 230) — that is also an open task.

8. **No data-residency commitment for the CASB's own data plane.** Line 58 acknowledges API CASB scanning is bound by the SaaS app's residency, not Palo Alto's. The draft does not address where Strata Logging Service stores logs, where Enterprise DLP profiles inspect content, where Behavior Threats baselines are computed, or how to satisfy a BNM RMiT 11.x cloud requirement that customer data and operational logs do not exit Malaysia without explicit approval. PDPA s.129 (cross-border transfer) requires a basis. This needs a region-by-region table, sourced from the Palo Alto data-residency / sub-processor list, with a vendor-DPA reference.

9. **Sub-processor / fourth-party disclosure missing.** WildFire (line 62, 138), Enterprise DLP cloud service, AI Access Security dictionary refresh (line 68) — each is a separate processing locus that under GDPR Art. 28(2)–(4) and PDPA's data-processor obligations needs sub-processor disclosure, contractual flow-down, and change notification. The draft is silent.

10. **Policy #2 quarantine action (lines 86–92, 158–188) has no privacy guardrail.** "Admin Quarantine (copy to admin folder, tombstone original)" means a DLP operator now sees the contents of a user's externally-shared file. If that file is personal data, health data, or content under legal privilege, the quarantine action itself is a processing event with its own lawful-basis question. GDPR Art. 32 + Art. 5(1)(f) (integrity and confidentiality), ISO/IEC 27018 A.10.13 (return, transfer, disposal of PII). The "exclude: owners_in_group: legal-counsel" line (line 176) is the right instinct; it is not sufficient. Add: a documented quarantine-review workflow with two-person integrity, an audit log of who viewed quarantined content, and a retention/deletion clock on the admin folder.

11. **Behavior Threats baselines (90 days, line 54) are personal data.** Per-user hourly download volume, off-hours patterns, location patterns — under GDPR these are profiling within Art. 22 territory and need DPIA + employee notice. Under PDPA they are personal data processing under s.6. The draft treats this as a feature, not a regulated activity. The compliance overlay needed: lawful basis, retention period on the baseline itself, employee transparency, and a documented procedure for handling false positives without disciplinary consequence.

12. **OAuth revoke (Policy #6, lines 118–124) lacks change-management and reversibility evidence.** Auto-revoking a third-party OAuth grant affects user productivity and, where the grant supports a business process, business operations. BNM RMiT 10.x change-management expectations apply to security-tool-driven configuration changes too. Add: pre-revoke notification path, owner approval threshold above a productivity-impact line, rollback playbook, and a sign-off artefact.

13. **No statement of which logs are themselves auditable evidence and which are operational only.** Strata Logging Service is named (lines 17, 36) but the draft does not state retention period, immutability mode (append-only / WORM), tamper-evident sealing, export format, or whether the customer can subpoena the vendor's own access logs to the customer tenant (a HKMA SA-2 / MAS TRM Outsourcing Notice expectation for cloud service providers handling regulated data).

14. **No CSA CCM or CAIQ reference.** For a CASB the Cloud Security Alliance CCM v4 / CAIQ-Lite is the lingua franca that lets a customer GRC team request a structured attestation. Absent that reference, the practitioner has nothing to hand to procurement / vendor risk.

15. **No statement on lawful-intercept / government-access disclosure posture.** Under MAS TRM and HKMA SA-2 the institution must understand vendor government-access posture (transparency reports, law-enforcement request handling, encryption-of-data-at-rest with customer-held keys vs. vendor-held). Silent.

## Capability claims to corroborate (do not promote without independent source)

- **"55+ of 80+ tracked attributes" risk scoring on Discovered Apps (line 44).** Vendor marketing number. Not corroborated; not material to compliance evidence unless the attribute set is auditable and stable across releases.
- **"~28 apps as of mid-2026" API-connector coverage (line 35).** Vendor count. Independent corroboration: customer-side list pulled at deployment time, dated.
- **"25 MB across all apps" hard ceiling (lines 50, 194, 228).** Asserted as universal; the author flags uncertainty about an enterprise tier. Pin down before publication — auditors will ask "what about the file that didn't get scanned?".
- **"90 days of historical data" Behavior Threats baseline (line 54).** Confirm retention, configurability, and what happens at tenant offboarding.
- **Tenant-restriction header support for Claude and Gemini (line 146).** Author already flags this. Compliance lens position: do not promote as a regulator-facing control until each app's documented header schema and Prisma Access dictionary entry are independently confirmed; otherwise Policy #9 is "best-effort".
- **"Forward-scan only" classification for Gmail / Chat / Zoom / Zendesk / Airtable / ChatGPT (lines 50, 195).** Author flags uncertainty. Materially affects historical-content compliance posture (e.g. "have you scanned what is already in Gmail?"). Confirm per app.
- **"No public independent CVE / breach record" (line 230).** Self-flagged. Run CVE / NVD / vendor advisory search before publication. Absence-of-record is not a substitute for "checked and found nothing".
- **WildFire's own attestations.** Whether WildFire as a sub-processor has its own SOC 2 / ISO 27001 / FedRAMP boundary distinct from Prisma Access. Material for the sub-processor disclosure point above.
- **Strata Logging Service region pinning + retention defaults.** Cite from the data-residency / sub-processor page, not from product marketing.

## Policies to refine

- **Policy #1 (block PCI/PII to unsanctioned GenAI):** add (a) BNM RMiT mapping (10.x cyber + 11.x cloud), PCI DSS v4.0.1 req 3 (protect stored cardholder data) + req 4 (transmission), ISO 27017 CLD.12.4.5; (b) the decryption-of-personal-traffic privacy guardrail described in finding 3; (c) log artefact spec: per-block log with policy ID, DLP pattern hit count, user, target app, timestamp, TLS-decryption-mode indicator.
- **Policy #2 (Google Drive PII quarantine):** add quarantine-review workflow per finding 10; add retention clock on the admin folder; add file-owner notification artefact; clarify whose lawful basis covers the quarantine processing.
- **Policy #3 (block SharePoint download to unmanaged):** HIP status is a device attribute — capture how device-posture data flow complies with BYOD privacy expectations and what happens on personal-device sessions to corporate SaaS (PDPA notice). Log artefact must include HIP attribute set evaluated.
- **Policy #4 (M365 tenant restriction):** named, low risk; add the auditor evidence — sample HTTP-header-insert log proving the enforcement actually fired, plus a periodic verification test (synthetic transaction).
- **Policy #5 (impossible travel Salesforce):** add the personal-data caveat from finding 11; document IP-exclusion-list ownership and review cadence; clarify incident-handling SLA tied to regulator clock.
- **Policy #6 (OAuth revoke):** add change-management wrapper per finding 12; named approver above an impact threshold; explicit allow-list ownership; revocation-event log retention.
- **Policy #7 (Box bulk download off-hours):** add the "off-hours" definition source (whose calendar, whose time zone, public-holiday handling); add the false-positive disposition path so an employee is not disciplined from an unverified ML alert; reference NIST CSF DE.AE-2 (analysed events) + RS.AN-1 (investigations).
- **Policy #8 (OneDrive malware scan):** 25 MB ceiling is a documented gap — add the compensating control (endpoint AV, M365 ATP, S/MIME on shared installers) and own the residual risk.
- **Policy #9 (sanctioned GenAI tenant):** see finding 6 + corroboration list. Until Claude / Gemini header enforcement is independently confirmed, this is "best-effort" and must be labelled as such.
- **Policy #10 (coach away from Tolerated apps):** coach pages are an awareness control under ISO 27001 A.6.3 / NIST CSF PR.AT — capture per-user impression metrics as the evidence artefact, not the policy existence itself.

## Limitations the draft missed

- **No retention / immutability statement for the audit logs that this CASB produces.** Strata Logging Service is the evidence pipeline; absent retention + WORM + integrity-attestation specifics, regulators in BNM/MAS/HKMA scope treat the logs as operational, not evidential.
- **No data-residency table for Prisma Access tenants, Strata Logging Service regions, Enterprise DLP processing locations, or AI Access Security dictionary refresh paths.**
- **No sub-processor list or vendor DPA reference.**
- **No independent attestation references (SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, CSA STAR, FedRAMP, PCI DSS Service Provider).**
- **No statement of vendor government-access / lawful-intercept posture.**
- **No employee-privacy / DPIA wrapper for SSL decryption, Behavior Threats baselining, or device-posture (HIP) telemetry — the three highest privacy-impact features of this product.**
- **No clear separation of "personal data processor" (Palo Alto) vs. "personal data controller" (the customer) roles under PDPA / GDPR for the Data Security at-rest scanning function, including transitive role for WildFire detonation of customer files.**
- **No statement on data subject access / erasure handling.** When a former employee exercises a PDPA s.30 access request, what does the customer extract from Strata Logging Service / Behavior Threats baselines / Data Security incident history? Process is undefined.
- **No CSA CCM / CAIQ reference.**
- **No statement of how policy changes themselves are logged.** Auditors will ask: "show me who changed the PII-Strict profile last quarter, when, why, with what approval". Strata Cloud Manager audit-of-admin-action specifics are absent.
- **No segregation-of-duties statement.** Who can edit a DLP profile, view quarantined content, revoke OAuth grants, modify Behavior Threats thresholds — and is that the same person? ISO 27001 A.5.3, BNM RMiT 10.x.
- **No statement on the customer's ability to detect a vendor-side configuration drift or unauthorised admin action by Palo Alto support staff on the customer tenant.** HKMA SA-2 / MAS TRM Outsourcing expectation.
- **Cross-border data flow for incident response.** When Palo Alto's TAC / SOC accesses a customer tenant for support, what data crosses what border, under what contract? Silent.
- **No retention / deletion specification at contract termination.** Off-boarding clock for logs, DLP profiles, Behavior Threats baselines, and quarantine admin folder.

## Overall verdict

- **Promote as-is to 04-vendors/?** NO
- **One-line reason:** The draft documents capability but evidences nothing — no control mapping, no log-artefact spec, no privacy guardrail on TLS decryption, no regulator timing, no independent attestation; revise before promotion.
